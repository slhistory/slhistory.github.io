Sithulpawwa

Sithulpawwa a rock temple also known as Chiththalapabbatha. Located within the boundaries ofYala National park. About 28 km from Tissa, this was built I 2nd century BC. by King Kavanthissa. Thousands of arahath monks and meditating monks a lived in this temple. This was a center of Buddhist education for Buddhist monks. There is number of stupas, cave, Buddha statues ponds located in temple premises. The main Sithulpawwa stupa was built by flattening the top of a rocky mountain

One of the caves contains ancient paintings of Anuradhapura era. These paintings done on a thin layerof plaster on the rock surface and primarily used red – yellow colours Inside of these caves’ condtions made suitable for the monks to live. There are number of small stupas found inside Situlpawwa rock temple.

The excavations on this area found many valuble items such as many boddhastues,staue of goddess thara Statues of the Awalokeswara Bodisattva According to the Historical evidence two of the ten giant warriors Gotaimbara and Phusssadeva legendary Ten lived in this area. There is a another hill called Small Sithulpawwa containing similar stupa and buildings
